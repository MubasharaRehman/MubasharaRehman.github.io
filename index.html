ReMAR-DS: Metal Artifact Reduction and Domain Transformation from kVCT to MVCT

Mubashara Rehman, Niki Martinel, Michele Avanzo, Riccardo Spizzo, Christian Micheloni

23rd International Conference on Image Analysis and Processing (ICIAP), 2025

Metal artifacts in kVCT affect clinical accuracy. We propose ReMAR-DS, an encoder–decoder framework with enhanced feature recalibration for metal artifact reduction and kVCT→MVCT domain transformation. By emphasizing artifact-prone regions and preserving anatomical structures, our method generates high-quality MVCT-like reconstructions, improving radiotherapy planning while reducing repeated high-dose scans.

Report on the Hands-On PhD Course on Responsible AI from the Lens of an Information Access Researcher

Damiano Spina, Kevin Roitero, Stefano Mizzaro, Vincenzo Della Mea, Francesca Da Ros, Michael Soprano, Hafsa Akebli, Alex Falcon, Mehdi Fasihi, Alessio Fiorin, David La Barbera, Daniele Lizzio Bosco, Riccardo Lunardi, Alberto Marturano, Zaka-Ud-Din Muhammad, Francesco Nascimben, Moritz Nottebaum, Massimiliano Pascoli, Mihai Horia Popescu, Laura Rasotto, Mubashara Rehman, Francesco Taverna, Biagio Tomasetig, Alessandro Tremamunno

ACM SIGIR Forum, Vol. 58, No. 2, pp. 1–61, March 2025

This paper presents a four-day PhD-level course on Responsible Artificial Intelligence, held at the University of Udine. Through case studies and interactive activities, the course explored concepts such as positionality, fairness, diversity, ethics, and participatory research in the context of information retrieval. With 23 participants from diverse academic backgrounds, the course highlighted practical ways to integrate responsible AI into research and education.

MAR-DTN: Metal Artifact Reduction Using Domain Transformation Network for Radiotherapy Planning

Belén Serrano-Antón, Mubashara Rehman, Niki Martinel, Michele Avanzo, Riccardo Spizzo, Giuseppe Fanetti, Alberto P. Muñuzuri, Christian Micheloni

Pattern Recognition (ICPR 2024), Lecture Notes in Computer Science, vol. 15311, Springer, Cham, December 2024

This study addresses the limitations of kVCT scans for radiotherapy planning, where metallic implants often cause severe artifacts. We propose a UNet-inspired domain transformation network (MAR-DTN) to generate artifact-free MVCT images from kVCT inputs, leveraging the strengths of both modalities. The method achieves PSNR of 30.02 dB across entire patient volumes and 27.47 dB in artifact-affected regions, enabling enhanced tissue contrast and more precise radiotherapy treatment calibration.

A Fast and Robust Embedded Optical Flow Unit on Motion Estimation

Mubashara Rehman, Chung Ming Own, Ziqi Li

Proceedings of the 2021 10th International Conference on Software and Computer Applications (ICSCA '21), Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, pp. 180–185, ACM, 2021

We propose a fast and robust optical flow estimation unit (FUOF) that enhances FlowNetC with correlation-based feature matching, an Extended Sobel operator for efficient spatial extraction, and two fusion strategies (FUOF-Sum and FUOF-Concat). Experiments on MPI Sintel and Flying Chairs datasets demonstrate competitive accuracy, faster convergence, and robustness compared to existing methods.
